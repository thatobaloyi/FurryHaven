The Five Freedoms of Animal Welfare

The Five Freedoms are internationally recognized principles ensuring animals live healthy, happy, and dignified lives. Explore them below.

Freedom from Hunger and Thirst

Providing ready access to fresh water and a diet to maintain full health and vigour.

Freedom from Discomfort

Providing an appropriate environment, including shelter from the elements.

Freedom from Pain, Injury and Disease

Ensuring access to veterinary care and preventing injury or disease.

Freedom to Express Normal Behaviour

Providing sufficient space, proper facilities, and company of their own kind.

Freedom from Fear and Distress

Ensuring conditions and treatment which avoid mental suffering.
